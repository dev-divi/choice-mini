Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
The Basic Idea
In the first 30 minutes after the market opens, a trading range gets established — a high and a low. That's the "opening range."
The Strategy

If price breaks above the high → you buy
If price breaks below the low → you sell short

The theory is that whichever direction price breaks out of that range, it tends to keep going in that direction for the rest of the day.
Why It Works (Sometimes)

The open is the most volatile, news-heavy part of the day
Big institutional orders are getting filled
Once those settle, the "real" direction of the day reveals itself
The breakout signals which side won

Simple Example

Market opens, stock bounces between $100 and $102 for 30 minutes
At 10am it breaks above $102
You buy, targeting $104-$105
You set a stop loss back at $100 in case it reverses

The Catch

Lots of fake breakouts — price pokes above/below then reverses
Works better on high volume, volatile stocks/days
Requires discipline on stop losses

It's one of the most popular day trading strategies because it's simple and has a clear entry signal.
